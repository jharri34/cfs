Neuroinflammation Pain and Fatigue Lab

We are conducting a novel research study in which we try to identify biological markers for Chronic Fatigue Syndrome/ Systemic Exertion Intolerance Disease. Identifying a marker may ultimately help us understand the mechanism of these illnesses and thus develop better treatments. This has never been looked at before. In order to answer this question, we will need to collect blood samples and see how that relates to your symptoms. The study would involve coming to University of Alabama at Birmingham (UAB). You will be compensated for your time. Thank you for your interest in our research.

About our staff:

Dr. Jarred Younger-

Director of Neuroinflammation Pain and Fatigue Lab Jarred Younger received his Ph.D. in Experimental Psychophysiology in 2003 at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville. He then completed postdoctoral fellowships at Arizona State University and the Stanford University School of Medicine before taking an assistant professor position at Stanford. In 2014, he joined the faculty at the University of Alabama Birmingham, with a primary appointment in the Department of Psychology and secondary appointments in the Departments of Anesthesiology and Rheumatology. Prof. Younger's goal is to end the chronic pain and fatigue that is caused by inflammation in the brain. He is currently funded by the National Institutes of Health, Department of Defense, and several non-profit agencies to develop techniques for diagnosing and treating neuroinflammation, pain, and fatigue.
